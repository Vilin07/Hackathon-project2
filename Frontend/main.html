Success Stories

Shri Mahila Griha Udyog - Lijjat Papad

When 7 ladies from a building gathered on a terrace in Girgaon, Southern Mumbai, they gave birth to a new concept of entrepreneurship. Started in 1959, Lijjat Papad became a women-led cooperative with over 50% market share in India. Their success is a testament to the power of unity and determination, with an annual turnover of ₹1600 crores and 45,000+ women employed nationwide.

Kanika Talukar – Jay Vermi Compost

After losing her husband at 27, Kanika from Assam started a vermicomposting business with just ₹500 and 1 kg earthworms. She now earns ₹3.5 lakh/month and sells her products on Flipkart and Amazon. Her story shows how resilience and local resources can build a thriving green business.

Bina Devi – The Mushroom Lady of Bihar

Bina Devi from Munger, Bihar learned mushroom cultivation and started under her bed due to space issues. She trained 1500+ women in 105 villages and was awarded the Nari Shakti Samman. She symbolizes women-led agricultural transformation in rural India.

Pabiben Rabari – Pabiben.com

Pabiben from Gujarat invented 'Hari Jari' embroidery and created the iconic 'Pabi Bag', featured in Bollywood & Hollywood. Today, she runs a D2C brand with over ₹2 crore in sales and appeared on Shark Tank India. Her story shows how tradition can lead to global success.
